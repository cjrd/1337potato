1337: reacting is easier

It’s easier to let other people occupy my time. React to their needs.
It feels productive to make progress for others. To see that I can help them.
Responding to emails, attending meetings, looking at Slack alerts.

So I sit down to do some deep work: some heavy thought about big projects. And I find myself checking my email and looking for Slack alerts. Secretly hoping someone else will block out the next hour for me, so that I don’t have to drive forward my own thoughts. Because it’s hard to do that. It hurts to do that. I feel incompetent and inadequate when I do that.

But letting this happen leads to a busy, ostensibly productive life of doing very little. But as a declaration of focus, let me say that I will choose my time, and explicitly accept that it will be more difficult to do this. I will have to let others down, and I will likely fail quite a bit. But it’s better than the alternative.

Now let’s get started on some of those future failing ideas.
