1337: rain on leaves

Rain began to beat down on me yesterday morning.

I was trail running.
I woke up before the sun.
My mind was clear.
I was doing my part.

But the rain beat down on my path. It began to pour, and I could hear the orchestra of the raining forest: rain on leaves.

And when I think about the sound of rain on leaves, I think of relaxation. I imagine most people do. It’s used in spas and healing centers, white noise machines and concentration soundtracks.

But I was wet, and my fingers were numb. It wasn’t a spa or a sleep aid. It was uncomfortable. The rain on the leaves was uncomfortable. I had every reason to feel uncomfortable, to put my mind in a state of discomfort and feel sorry for my bad luck.

I had every reason to feel sorry for my unfortunate circumstances.

Or.
I could listen to the rain on the leaves.
